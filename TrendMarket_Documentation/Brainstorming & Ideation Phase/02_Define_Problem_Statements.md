## 1. Introduction
A well-defined problem statement ensures that developer efforts focus on building features that address the precise pain points of the target audience. For the **TrendMarket** platform, we have defined two problem statements: one from the customer's (buyer's) perspective, and another from the administrator's (business owner's) perspective.

---

## 2. Customer Problem Statement

### 2.1 The Customer Profile
* **User Type:** Urban consumers, tech-savvy shoppers, and fashion/lifestyle trend-followers.
* **Goals:** Quick browsing of the latest items, secure and fast transactions, transparent stock details, and easy tracking of order statuses.

### 2.2 The Customer Problem
"I am a trend-conscious online shopper who wants a fast, responsive, and secure marketplace to buy fashion items. However, I am frequently frustrated by websites that let me add out-of-stock items to my cart, force me through insecure or complex checkout flows, and do not provide clear details about my past orders."

### 2.3 Why It is a Problem
* **Waste of Time:** Searching for products only to discover they are unavailable at checkout is discouraging.
* **Security Anxieties:** E-commerce sites that use weak local-storage auth make users anxious about the safety of their profiles and payment histories.
* **Lack of Transparency:** Not knowing whether an order was successful or what its status is leads to customer support overhead and cart abandonment.

### 2.4 Customer Emotional Impact
Customers feel **frustrated** by sluggish loading speeds, **insecure** about entering details, and **disappointed** when their expectations of a smooth digital transaction are unmet.

## 3. Merchant / Administrator Problem Statement

### 3.1 The Merchant Profile
* **User Type:** Small-to-medium retail owners, e-commerce administrators, and warehouse/inventory managers.
* **Goals:** Efficiently list new trend items, monitor live stock counts, review incoming orders, and track revenue growth.

### 3.2 The Merchant Problem
"I am an online store manager who needs to keep our digital catalog aligned with our real physical inventory. Currently, I have to manually inspect stock, use disjointed databases to verify customer orders, and lack a single dashboard to view sales analytics or manage listings."

### 3.3 Why It is a Problem
* **Administrative Overhead:** Manually matching payments with orders and updating inventory values takes hours.
* **Sales Loss:** If an item is sold out but remains listed, customers cancel orders, hurting reputation.
* **Blind Decision-Making:** Without centralized sales reporting, it is impossible to know which items are trending and should be restocked.

### 3.4 Merchant Emotional Impact
Administrators feel **overwhelmed** by manual database tasks, **disorganized** due to fragmented operations, and **fearful** of human error causing delivery delays.
